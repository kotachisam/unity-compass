The **Irish Citizens' Assembly** refers to a series of randomly-selected citizen deliberation processes held in Ireland from 2012 onwards. Two assemblies produced recommendations that led to successful constitutional referenda: marriage equality (2015) and abortion access (2018). Both were deeply divisive issues that elected politicians had avoided for decades.

## The mechanism

99 randomly selected citizens met over multiple weekends, heard from experts representing diverse perspectives, deliberated in small groups with trained facilitators and voted on recommendations. The government committed in advance to act on recommendations through referendum.

## Why it matters for the investigation

The investigation maps this as coordination under [[Value Incommensurability]]. The participants held genuinely different values about human life, sexuality and the role of religion in public life. These values were not reconcilable on a common scale. The assembly did not resolve the value disagreement. It created conditions under which citizens could coordinate on a recommendation despite the disagreement, because the process itself was perceived as legitimate.

## What made it work

- **Sortition** removed electoral incentives: participants were not performing for voters or donors
- **Structured deliberation** gave time for positions to evolve through genuine engagement rather than strategic positioning
- **Expert input from multiple perspectives** meant participants encountered the strongest versions of views they disagreed with
- **Small-group facilitation** created conditions where perspective-taking and tension-holding could actually operate
- **Government commitment** gave the process legitimacy and stakes

## Transferable insight

[[Value Incommensurability]] can be navigated (though not resolved) when the coordination process itself is trusted. Legitimacy of mechanism can substitute for convergence of values. If the people involved believe the process is fair, they can accept outcomes they disagree with. This suggests that one bottleneck for coordination under incommensurability may be the absence of trusted process rather than the absence of shared values.

## Limitations

Ireland is a small, relatively culturally cohesive nation. The government honoured the recommendations. Scaling to contexts with less social trust, larger populations, or governments without the political will to bind themselves to citizen recommendations remains an open question.
