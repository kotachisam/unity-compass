**Value incommensurability** describes the condition where two or more values cannot be ranked on a common scale. They are not equal (choosing between them is not a matter of indifference), nor is one straightforwardly better or worse than the other. They are, in [[Ruth Chang]]'s terminology, "on a par": comparable but not rankable.

## Formal significance

Standard [[Evolutionary Game Theory]] requires a constructible payoff matrix. When agents hold genuinely incommensurable values, no common payoff matrix can be constructed, because the agents cannot agree on the ranking of outcomes. This means that the dominant formal framework for analysing coordination has no starting point for the cases the investigation cares about most.

## Examples

Value incommensurability appears at every scale of human coordination:

- A couple where one partner values security and the other values adventure, and neither value is "more" of anything the other recognises
- A dispute between indigenous land rights and economic development where the very concept of what land *is* differs categorically
- A nation that defines security as territorial sovereignty negotiating with one that defines it as economic interdependence

In each case, the parties are not disagreeing about how much of a shared value to pursue. They are operating with genuinely different evaluative frameworks.

## The spectrum

Fully commensurable and fully incommensurable values represent the extremes. The space between, partial commensurability, represents the vast majority of existing conditions. The investigation's research proposal argues that coordination theory should address this graduated middle ground formally, rather than defaulting to either extreme.

## Chang's voluntarist account

[[Ruth Chang]] argues that when individual agents face hard choices between incommensurable options, they resolve them through volitional commitment: constituting themselves as the kind of person who values X over Y, rather than discovering a pre-existing ranking. Whether collectives can exercise analogous agency is one of the investigation's central questions.
