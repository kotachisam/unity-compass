# Case Studies (Theory)

Analytical examinations of real-world coordination across difference: what happened, what mechanisms were at play, what patterns recur, and what open questions remain.

These case studies provide the evidence base for the claims made in the [landscape analysis](../). Each one is referenced by at least one analytical document in the parent directory, which is noted in the case study's own header.

## Cases

**Successes**
- [The Montreal Protocol](montreal-protocol.md). International coordination on ozone depletion. The gold standard.
- [Irish Citizens' Assembly](irish-citizens-assembly.md). Sortition-based deliberative democracy producing constitutional change.
- [Open-Source Software Governance](open-source-software-governance.md). Coordination without convergence at global scale, built on protocol rather than ideology.

**Failures**
- [Fukushima Daiichi](fukushima-daiichi.md). Compounding institutional failure modes in nuclear safety.
- [2008 Financial Crisis](2008-financial-crisis.md). The accountability void and regulatory capture in global finance.
- [COVID-19 Response](covid-19-response.md). Real-time stress test of coordination at every scale simultaneously.

**In Progress**
- [AUKUS](aukus-trilateral-agreement.md). Trilateral coordination across different strategic cultures at nuclear-submarine stakes.

## Structure

Each case study follows a common format: summary, why it matters for the investigation, key patterns observed, open questions, and sources to consult. They are reference material rather than a reading sequence. Arrive from whichever analytical document pointed you here.
