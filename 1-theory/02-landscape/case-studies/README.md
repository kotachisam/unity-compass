# Case Studies (Theory)

Analytical examinations of real-world coordination across difference: what happened, what mechanisms were at play, what patterns connect to the framework, and what open questions remain.

These case studies provide the evidence base for the claims made in the [landscape analysis](../). Each one is referenced by at least one analytical document in the parent directory.

For the experiential dimension of these cases — what coordination across difference felt like from the inside, what capacities were required, the phenomenology rather than the analysis — see the [practice-spine case studies](../../../2-practice/03-case-studies/).

## Cases

**Successes**
- [The Montreal Protocol](montreal-protocol.md) — International coordination on ozone depletion. The gold standard.
- [Irish Citizens' Assembly](irish-citizens-assembly.md) — Sortition-based deliberative democracy producing constitutional change.
- [Open-Source Governance](open-source-governance.md) — Coordination without convergence at global scale, built on protocol rather than ideology.

**Failures**
- [Fukushima Daiichi](fukushima.md) — Compounding institutional failure modes in nuclear safety.
- [2008 Financial Crisis](2008-financial-crisis.md) — The accountability void and regulatory capture in global finance.
- [COVID-19 Response](covid-19-response.md) — Real-time stress test of coordination at every scale simultaneously.

**In Progress**
- [AUKUS](aukus.md) — Trilateral coordination across different strategic cultures at nuclear-submarine stakes.

## Structure

Each case study follows a common format: summary, why it matters for the framework, key patterns observed, open questions, and sources to consult. They are reference material rather than a reading sequence — arrive from whichever analytical document pointed you here.

Case studies are among the most valuable contributions the community can make. If you have operational experience of coordination across difference at any scale and want to write it up, see [CONTRIBUTING.md](../../../.github/CONTRIBUTING.md).
