# Written Work Sample

---
**Applicant:** Samuel Norton
**Course:** MPhil/PhD Philosophy
**Institution:** London School of Economics
---

> **Central claim.** Coordination theory's foundational assumption that agents' different values must be commensurable enough for aggregation is a useful theoretical scaffold but a vastly inaccurate model of coordination as it actually occurs. I motivate this by interrogating the modelling tradition behind the assumption — asking why its scope was constrained to cases admitting well-defined preferences, and where that scope ceases to track the coordination problems it purports to model. I then articulate the criterion any account of the structural middle would have to meet, illustrate the criterion with candidate features drawn from a paradigm case, and reply to the strongest live objection: that Sen's existing maximisation framework already does the work the structural middle thesis claims for itself. Systematic proof and development of the structural features themselves is the dissertation's work; the essay's contribution is the model-adequacy claim, the criterion, and the demonstration that plausible candidates exist.

---

## 1. Introduction

*Target: ~400 words*

**Purpose.** State the contribution in the opening paragraph. Three sentences maximum for the claim itself, then motivate. Frame the puzzle directly: coordination among agents whose values are not commensurable in the ways standard coordination theory assumes. Name the commensurability assumption explicitly. Signal the argument structure: interrogate the modelling tradition behind the assumption, steelman the assumption via its strongest contemporary defenders, show where it fits poorly, articulate the structural middle, reply to the Sen-adequacy challenge. Close with a brief roadmap. The essay stands alone — the committee reader who has only the sample (not the proposal) should not feel context is missing.

**Note on parallel literatures.** Use the intra-agent commensurability debate (Chang, Rabinowicz, Sen, Broome, Carlson) as evidence that even the more local version of the commensurability assumption has been substantively contested. This is foundational background, not the thesis being extended. The move here is at the inter-agent level, parallel to but distinct from the intra-agent debate.

## 2. The Commensurability Assumption and Its Theoretical Role

*Target: ~700 words*

**Purpose.** Steelman the commensurability assumption via its strongest contemporary defenders, then raise the prior modelling-presupposition question that primes §3. The primary engagement target for the steelman is Dorr, Nebel and Zuehl 2022 *The Case for Comparability* (Noûs). Their argument is the most rigorous recent articulation of why coordination requires commensurability at some level: they show that plausible structural axioms force comparability, and that incomparability claims collapse under formal analysis. Engaging their specific argument is more rigorous than engaging a generic "utilitarian aggregation" position, and signals philosophical capability to a careful reader. Klocksiem 2010 *In Defense of the Trichotomy Thesis* and Arrow's *Social Choice and Individual Values* (1951) are supporting context but DNZ is the live interlocutor.

**Sub-section: cardinality versus comparability.** Drawing on Sen Chapter 8 (Cardinality With or Without Comparability), separate the cardinality assumption (values have numerical magnitudes) from the comparability assumption (values can be related across agents). These are often run together. The thesis attacks the comparability assumption specifically. Cardinality without comparability is one limit; comparability without cardinality is another. The structural middle is where most real coordination operates.

**Sub-section: the modelling-presupposition question (Cartwright lineage).** Game theory and the social-choice tradition simplified their scope to cases admitting well-defined (typically cardinal) preferences. Ask two questions in the Cartwright applicability-conditions register, not the flaw-claim register: (i) why was the scope constrained so heavily — tractability or faithfulness to the phenomenon? (ii) at what stage does the assumption cease to track the coordination decisions the theory purports to model? The questions are not assertions that game theory is wrong. They are operationalisation questions whose answers prime §3. The empirical answer to (ii) is what §3 supplies; §4 names what lies on the other side of the line.

**Literature to cite.** DNZ 2022 (primary steelman); Klocksiem 2010 (supporting); Arrow 1951 (canonical source); utilitarian aggregation (Sidgwick, Mill) as background; Sen *Collective Choice and Social Welfare* Chapters 7, 7\*, 8 (formal apparatus reference); Cartwright *How the Laws of Physics Lie* (1983) and *The Dappled World* (1999) for the modelling-presupposition lineage that authorises the §2-§3 register.

## 3. The Commensurability Assumption as a Model: Where It Fits Poorly

*Target: ~700 words*

**Purpose.** The empirical move. Paradigm case (AUKUS) of coordination that does not fit the commensurability assumption. Make the demonstration structure explicit, in three steps:

1. **What the commensurability assumption predicts.** Effective coordination requires either a common payoff structure or a way to map agents' values onto a common scale. Without one, aggregation cannot be defined, and coordination through aggregation cannot proceed.
2. **What AUKUS coordination actually looks like.** Three nations operating under qualitatively different conceptions of regional security, no constructible common payoff structure, no canonical scale on which their security values can be aggregated.
3. **The mismatch.** Coordination occurs and persists despite the absence of the commensurability conditions standard theory says are required. The DNZ-style absorption move (treat this as a partial-commensurability case) is the obvious response; flag it here, develop the reply in §5.

**Literature to cite.** Chang *Comparativism: The Ground of Rational Choice* (2016) for the meta-claim that comparative facts ground rational choice; Carlson and Risberg "In Defense of Value Incomparability" (Noûs 2024) for the live state of the philosophical debate; AUKUS-specific policy literature for the operational case detail.

## 4. The Structural Middle on the Commensurability Axis

*Target: ~700 words*

**Purpose.** Constructive philosophical articulation, at the right scope. The essay's contribution here is not categorical defence of any specific feature as the load-bearing content of the structural middle. That is the dissertation's systematic work. The contribution here is twofold: (i) establish the criterion any structural feature must meet to count as content of the middle, and (ii) illustrate the criterion with candidate features drawn from the AUKUS case.

**Sub-section: the criterion (~150 words).** A structural feature of the middle is something that operates as a coordination ground irreducibly to commensurability-based aggregation. Specifically: a feature F counts as content of the structural middle on the commensurability axis if (a) F can be specified without presupposing that agents' values are commensurable, (b) F does coordination work in cases where the commensurability assumption fits poorly, and (c) the work F does cannot be recovered by any decomposition that absorbs F into a partial-commensurability framing. The criterion is the philosophical move; the candidates are illustrations of what meets it.

**Candidate 1: shared risk topology (~250 words).** Agents face entangled risks (security, ecological, infrastructural) that create coordination pressure without requiring shared evaluation of those risks on a common scale. The risks are entangled regardless of how agents value them. Develop the AUKUS case as worked example: the three nations face entangled strategic risks in the Indo-Pacific that bind their decisions structurally, regardless of how their respective doctrines value those risks. Make the case that this is a plausible candidate meeting the criterion: (a) specifiable without commensurability, (b) appears to do coordination work in the AUKUS case, (c) appears irreducible to a partial-commensurability decomposition because the entanglement structure itself, not the value-evaluations of it, does the binding.

**Candidate 2: procedural commitments (~250 words).** Shared procedural norms (treaty obligations, joint decision protocols, escalation procedures) mediate value comparisons without requiring substantive commensurability. Develop the AUKUS case again briefly: the trilateral procedural architecture binds the three agents even when their underlying security values diverge. Make the parallel case that this is a plausible candidate meeting the criterion.

**Close (~50 words).** These are two illustrations of the kind of thing the structural middle plausibly contains. Systematic proof — that these features actually do load-bearing coordination work irreducibly, and that they are the right ones to attend to — is the dissertation's work. The essay's contribution is the criterion plus the demonstration that it has plausible instances worth investigating.

**Literature to cite.** Schelling *The Strategy of Conflict* (1960) for shared risk topology and focal points; Ostrom *Governing the Commons* (1990) for procedural commitments; Alexander *The Structural Evolution of Morality* (2007) for emergent coordination norms; Bradley 2022 "Impartial Evaluation under Ambiguity" (the "left for another day" hook on incomplete betterness relations).

## 5. Objection and Reply: Does Sen Already Do This?

*Target: ~500 words*

**Purpose.** The strongest objection your actual LSE readers will fire at you, given Brown's published work on aggregation and Bradley's framework. The objection:

> Sen's maximisation framework already supplies what the structural middle thesis claims for itself. Quasi-orderings plus the maximal-set criterion handle exactly the partial-commensurability cases described above. Agents with incomplete orderings can rationally choose any option not strictly dominated. The structural middle on the commensurability axis is just Sen's terrain re-described.

This objection is sharper than the absorption objection because it engages your formal apparatus directly. State it cleanly and at strength.

**Reply.** Sen's framework treats incompleteness as **permissive**. It allows rational choice under incomplete orderings without making the normative claim that some incompleteness is ineliminable, or that the structural features that operate in the partial-commensurability middle are the substantive ground of coordination. Sen's maximal-set criterion answers the question "what can a rational agent choose under incompleteness?" The structural middle thesis answers a different question: "what features of inter-agent interdependence ground coordination when standard commensurability conditions fail?" The first is a descriptive permissiveness result; the second is a normative-constructive claim about coordination's structure.

Sen's framework is a necessary part of the apparatus this thesis uses. Partial orderings without completeness is the formal home for the inter-agent claim. But Sen alone does not characterise the inter-agent middle. He permits choice within it. Walk this through using the AUKUS case from §3: Sen's maximisation lets each agent choose rationally, but does not explain why the three agents coordinate on the joint outcomes they do. Candidate features meeting the §4 criterion (such as shared risk topology and procedural commitments) do explanatory work that Sen's permissive framework leaves out.

**Secondary objection (absorption).** "The structural middle is just partial commensurability under a different name." Reply: the partial-commensurability framing forces a decomposition of value space into commensurable and incommensurable parts. Features meeting the §4 criterion do not sit on a commensurability axis at all; the case made in §4 is that they are orthogonal to it. If that case holds, the absorption move has nowhere to land.

**Tertiary objection (Schelling/Ostrom citing-move register).** "Schelling and Ostrom already observed structural features that ground coordination without value convergence (focal points; institutional design). The structural middle is just what they were already doing." Reply (soft, citing-move): neither takes up the question of whether the underlying incompleteness in agents' evaluations is ineliminable. Schelling works within game theory's well-defined-preferences tradition, which is silent on the question. Ostrom is empirical-institutionalist and positionally agnostic on the value-theoretic substrate. Their observations are compatible with both the trichotomy-thesis frame (features as contingent workarounds inside a commensurability-presupposing model) and the structural-middle frame (features as substantive content of a distinct coordination topology). The essay's contribution is to ask the ineliminability question they do not, and to argue the latter framing is more adequate to the cases. One paragraph, not a full reply — the move is to extend what they did, not to displace it.

**Literature to cite.** Sen 1997 "Maximization and the Act of Choice" and *Collective Choice and Social Welfare* Chapter 7\* for the maximisation framework; DNZ 2022 for the absorbing-into-comparability move; Schelling *The Strategy of Conflict* (1960) Ch. 1 and Ostrom *Understanding Institutional Diversity* (2005) Ch. 1 for the citing-move reply.

## 6. Conclusion

*Target: ~200 words*

**Purpose.** Restate the claim and the contribution. The commensurability assumption is a useful theoretical scaffold; the empirical case is that it fits reality's coordination challenges poorly where they matter most. The structural middle is a substantive territory with its own properties, not a midpoint on a commensurability axis. The contribution of this essay is the model-adequacy claim, the criterion any account of the middle would have to meet, the demonstration that plausible candidates exist, and the case that Sen's maximisation framework, while necessary as apparatus, does not by itself characterise the inter-agent middle. Systematic proof and development is the dissertation's work. Close with a brief signal that the broader research direction extends the same model-adequacy register to adjacent assumptions in coordination theory — without naming or committing to the three-axis programme, which is the proposal's territory.

## Drafting guardrails

- The sample stands alone. The committee reader who has only the sample (not the proposal) should not feel context is missing. Do not announce a three-axis programme.
- "Is false" is metaphysical; "is a vastly inaccurate model" is empirical. The move stays empirical.
- AUKUS is evidence, not illustration. The case is load-bearing in §3 and revisited in §4 and §5.
- The Cartwright lineage runs through §2 and §3. Interrogate the modelling presupposition; do not assert flaws. Ask why the scope was constrained and where it loses relevance — let §3 answer the second question empirically.
- The intra-agent commensurability debate (Chang, Sen, DNZ, Carlson/Risberg) is referenced as parallel motivation in §1 and §2, not as the thesis being extended.
- "Structural middle" is the working term. Let the citable name emerge from §4 if it wants to. Do not introduce nomenclature ("collective comparativism" or similar) at sample scope unless it earns its keep.
- No formal apparatus. The sample is a philosophy paper, not a decision-theory paper.
- The civilisational stake stays implicit. The work itself is the stake.
- §2 steelmans DNZ 2022 specifically rather than the generic assumption. Specific interlocutor over generic position.
- §4 establishes the criterion any structural feature must meet, then illustrates with two candidates. The candidates are not categorically defended as the load-bearing content of the middle — that is the dissertation's systematic work. The essay's contribution is the criterion plus the illustration that it has plausible instances.
- §5 has three prongs: Sen-adequacy (primary, ~300 words), DNZ-absorption (secondary, ~100 words), Schelling/Ostrom-already-noticed (tertiary, citing-move register, ~100 words). Different objections; different replies. Do not merge them.
- "Candidate features" language throughout, not "the structural features." The honest scope is illustrative-at-doctoral-standard, not categorical.
- The essay makes the case for the claim. It does not prove it. The verb register is "argues for," "motivates," "illustrates," "makes the case for" — not "proves," "demonstrates conclusively," "establishes." Doctoral-standard rigour at 3000 words is rigorous case-making, not proof. Reserve proof language for the dissertation.
