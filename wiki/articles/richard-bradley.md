**Richard Bradley** is Professor of Philosophy at the London School of Economics and Political Science. His research focuses on decision theory, formal epistemology, social choice, scientific uncertainty and conditionals.

## Research focus

Bradley's *Decision Theory with a Human Face* (Cambridge UP) brings together his work on decision under ambiguity, belief revision and social choice. His paper "Social Ethics under Ambiguity" (*Ethics*, 2022) evaluates social prospects from an impartial standpoint when standard expected utility calculation breaks down.

He has an ongoing collaboration with Kai Spiekermann (Professor of Political Philosophy, LSE Department of Government) through the Open Society Foundation on cohesion under severe uncertainty. This cross-departmental collaboration is directly adjacent to the investigation's central question.

## Relevance to the investigation

Bradley is one of two target supervisors for the LSE MPhil/PhD application. His work is directly relevant to sub-question 3 of the research proposal: what does rational coordination require of agents who share [[Structural Interdependence]] but not values?

The investigation's key question for Bradley's framework: his "Social Ethics under Ambiguity" evaluates from an external, impartial standpoint. Coordination requires agents to act from within their own partial perspectives. The gap between impartial evaluation and situated coordination is where the thesis operates. Can Bradley's framework for [[Decision Theory Under Severe Uncertainty]] be extended from impartial evaluation to situated, first-person coordination?
