The **Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF)** coordinates thousands of engineers across the planet on the technical standards that make the internet function. It does this without voting, without binding authority and without requiring agreement. Its operating principle is "rough consensus and running code."

## Rough consensus

Rough consensus means the working group has addressed substantive objections, not that everyone agrees. The distinction matters. The IETF does not aim for unanimity. It aims for a state where remaining objections are understood, have been taken seriously, and are judged by the group to be outweighed by the value of proceeding. Dissenters can record their objections formally without blocking progress.

This resolves a specific pathology of consensus-based systems: the ability of a small minority to veto progress. It does so without majority-rule voting, which would risk railroading substantive objections.

## Coordination taxonomy mapping

IETF coordination is primarily pure coordination with assurance elements. The engineers want interoperable standards (pure coordination) and need confidence that the process will not be captured by a single corporate interest (assurance). Rough consensus provides the focal point while protecting against capture.

## Why the investigation cares

"Rough consensus" is a mechanism that deserves serious examination for coordination under [[Value Incommensurability]]. The principle that remaining objections can be acknowledged without requiring resolution, and that progress can proceed alongside documented disagreement, is precisely the kind of mechanism that coordination across genuine difference at larger scales might require. The IETF proves that you can take objections seriously and move forward at the same time. That is not compromise. It is a different kind of coordination.

## Limitations

IETF participants share a deep commitment to making the internet work. The shared artifact provides the focal point. Extending rough consensus to domains where actors lack that underlying commitment, or where the "artifact" is something as diffuse as a habitable planet, is genuinely untested.
