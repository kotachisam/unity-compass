**Coordination without convergence** is the central concept of the Unity Compass investigation. It refers to the proposal that collective coordination among agents can be grounded in structural features of their interdependence, assessed from each agent's situated perspective, rather than in shared values, aligned incentives, impartial evaluation or ideological agreement.

## The claim

The claim has two levels. The first is philosophical and rests on existing results. Value convergence is not sufficient for coordination: [[Mancur Olson]] showed that shared interests do not reliably produce collective action, because strategic structures can block cooperation even when all parties would benefit. Value convergence is also not necessary: [[Elinor Ostrom]] showed that coordination can emerge through institutional design among actors who do not share values, and [[Thomas Schelling]] showed that coordination can emerge through structural salience without shared values or explicit communication. If convergence is neither sufficient nor necessary, the question is what else can ground coordination.

The second level is the investigation's own sharpening of this question into a specific formal proposal. Coordination can be grounded in **structural features of interdependence assessed from situated perspectives**. Structural features are properties of the relational configuration: shared risk topology, entanglement density, constraint topology and procedural commitments. These features are observer-independent in a conditional sense. Given that agents share a vocabulary for what counts as a downside or an entanglement, the topology of those features is a fact about the arrangement rather than a feature of any particular agent's preferences. Situated assessment replaces impartial evaluation: each agent reads the structure from where they stand, and coordination stability is defined relative to those situated readings rather than to a view from nowhere.

## The diagnostic gap (Alexander 2024)

Alexander (2024) identifies the same gap from the diagnostic side. His argument against Popper's piecemeal social engineering is that rehabilitating the Open Society "involves global comparisons and trade-offs along dimensions not obviously comparable" (p. 16). Alexander's 346-page diagnostic describes precisely the problem the investigation's formal proposal addresses, but provides no formal object for making such comparisons. His concept of Rorschach concepts, political slogans like "Make America Great Again" onto which individuals project divergent content, is a direct empirical illustration of coordination without convergence in sociological terms. See [[Rorschach Concepts]]. His proposed response, [[Multifaceted Social Engineering]], relies on a fitness landscape metaphor whose axes, fitness function and navigation method remain unspecified. The investigation proposes to give that metaphor formal content.

## The formal gap (Bradley 2022)

Bradley (2022) maps the gap from the formal side. He proves that three weak conditions, Outcome Anonymity, State Dominance and Pareto for Equivalent Outcomes, force restrictive results on impartial social evaluation under ambiguity: homogeneity of individual betterness, ambiguity neutrality and strong separability. These results hold even under assumptions weaker than any mainstream rationality theory. Bradley flags incomplete betterness relations as "left for another day" (Section VI). See [[Incomplete Preferences]].

The investigation proposes to walk through that door. The coordination space aims to preserve the structural analogues of State Dominance and Pareto while replacing impartial evaluation with situated structural assessment. The working claim is that this escapes the homogeneity and ambiguity-neutrality results, because those results are forced by impartiality specifically, not by dominance or Pareto as such. This is a working proposal, not a proven result.

## The formal programme

The investigation's formal direction, as of the current state, is a configuration space with structural and evaluative axes in the same manifold, distinguished by coordinate type rather than by separate layers. Structural coordinates are observer-independent, evaluative coordinates are agent-specific. Stability is defined by a structural equilibrium concept that generalises Nash to partial orderings over position-vectors, recovering classical Nash as the degenerate limit where partial orderings collapse to complete ones and the manifold collapses to a discrete strategy set. Parity (see [[Parity]]) appears as a substantive region of non-dominance rather than as a boundary case between "better" and "worse".

The mathematical home spans differential geometry (for the configuration manifold and its metric), convex analysis and cone theory (for the partial orderings), and vector-valued game theory (for the equilibrium concept). This is held as a working direction, not a proven result. The explicit mathematical sketch is held back from the written proposal and reserved for supervision discussion.

## Relationship to existing theory

Most coordination theory either assumes convergence (shared payoff structures in [[Evolutionary Game Theory]], common utility functions in mechanism design, interpersonally comparable well-being in Harsanyi-style social evaluation) or tries to produce it (deliberation in Habermasian democracy, negotiation in bargaining theory, shared intentions in Bratman's account of cooperative activity). The investigation argues there is a third path: coordination grounded in structural features of the interdependence itself, assessed from situated positions, without requiring or producing agreement on values. Bratman's conditions may succeed when they do not because shared intentions are the ground but because they co-occur with structural features that are.

## Scope

The investigation initially tests the hypothesis on multi-lateral defence coordination, specifically the [[AUKUS]] trilateral arrangement, where three nations with different strategic doctrines coordinate on catastrophic security risks without sharing a common payoff structure. Generalisation to climate governance, AI regulation and international financial coordination is the research programme beyond the thesis.
