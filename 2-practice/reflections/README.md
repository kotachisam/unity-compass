# Reflections

Thematic essays and thinking-log entries. First-person prose about coordination from lived experience. The primary home for the practice spine's thinking-log function.

## Purpose

This directory holds reflection-style writing: essays, observations, half-formed thoughts, memoir-style pieces, working-through of ideas that haven't yet resolved into formal claims. Content here is exploratory, iterative, sometimes vulnerable, sometimes contradictory with earlier entries. The goal is captured thinking, not polished output.

Where the [theory spine](../../1-theory/) makes arguments, reflections tell stories, surface patterns, sit with uncertainty and occasionally commit to observations that might later need revising. That's a feature.

## Convention

**Filename pattern**: `YYYY-MM-topic-slug.md` (e.g. `2026-04-hierarchy-and-judgment.md`). Date-prefixed so the trajectory of thinking stays legible in directory order.

**Voice**: first-person, honest, as vulnerable as the topic warrants. Analytical where that serves better. No requirement to reconcile tensions with earlier entries or with theory content; the whole point is working through.

**Format**: free-form. No required template. A reflection can be three paragraphs or three thousand words. It can close with a clean conclusion or trail off into open questions. Whatever the thought wants to be.

**Dating and revising**: entries carry their written date. If a reflection is substantially revisited later, add a dated addendum rather than overwriting the original. The trajectory matters.

## What Belongs Here

- Thematic reflections on coordination, culture, hierarchy, judgment, political pressure, institutional dynamics
- Observations from operational experience that don't yet need to be tied tightly to theory
- Working-through of ideas before they're formal enough for theory or the PhD proposal
- Personal essays on what coordinating under catastrophic uncertainty has taught
- Reversals of earlier thinking, flagged honestly
- Things the theory voice can't say because the theory voice has to be analytical

## What Doesn't Belong Here

- Formal theory claims (those go in [1-theory/](../../1-theory/))
- Bounded case analyses tied explicitly to theory claims (those go in [operational-insights/](../operational-insights/))
- Reading notes on primary sources (those go in [3-project/working-papers/reading/notes/](../../3-project/working-papers/reading/notes/))
- Proposal drafts (those go in [3-project/working-papers/proposals/](../../3-project/working-papers/proposals/))
