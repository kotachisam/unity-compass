# Design Principles

The previous documents established two things. [Where coordination theory runs out](04-philosophical-foundation.md): when actors hold genuinely different values and the standard toolkit of negotiation, aggregation and compromise can't bridge the gap. And what the formal toolkit leaves out: dimensions like empathy, tolerance for sustained tension, situated wisdom, intellectual humility and relational trust, all excluded from formal coordination theory by its philosophical inheritance, disciplinary origins and methodological constraints.

These design principles are the bridge between that diagnosis and the [frameworks](../03-frameworks/) that follow. Each one responds to a specific dimension of the problem and is shaped by the [barriers to implementation](03-why-not-already.md) that any real-world coordination mechanism would have to survive.

---

**1. Coordination without convergence.**
The framework must enable collective action between actors who hold genuinely different values, worldviews and goals. It must never require ideological agreement as a precondition for cooperation. Where convergence emerges, it should be a byproduct of coordination, not a prerequisite for it.

**2. Logos-breadth over logic-alone.**
The investigation keeps visible the dimensions of practical reason that formal logic excludes: situated knowledge, embodied wisdom, tolerance for contradiction, relational intelligence. Whether these dimensions can be incorporated into a structural account or whether they remain permanent residue is an open question. Treating them as invisible because they resist formalisation is not an option.

**3. Pluralism-preserving.**
The framework must protect genuine difference rather than eroding it. Coordination that works by gradually homogenising its participants has succeeded at cooperation but failed at pluralism. The goal is coordination that recognises diversity as the structural asset it is, rather than seeing it as an obstacle to be overcome.

**4. Operationalisable.**
Formal claims should eventually produce mechanisms that institutions can test, implement and evaluate. The investigation resists staying entirely abstract. Where a claim about coordination cannot be connected to something testable, that is itself a finding worth naming honestly rather than a hole to disguise.

**5. Scale-aware, not yet scale-independent.**
Observed coordination patterns recur across scales: between individuals, within teams, across organisations, between nations. The long-term ambition is a formal account that generalises across these scales. Current work begins at one scale (trilateral state coordination) with the explicit task of testing whether what holds there also holds elsewhere. Scale-independence is an aspiration, not a near-term claim.

**6. Barrier-aware.**
The investigation holds the [barriers to implementation](03-why-not-already.md) explicitly in mind. Academically, by pursuing doctoral work aimed at peer review. Practically, by testing claims against operational experience of the systems being theorised about. Publicly, so the thinking stays accessible outside the rooms where coordination theory has usually been trapped. If a claim cannot survive contact with the institutional realities documented in this repository, it needs revision.

**7. Honest about incompleteness.**
The framework acknowledges what it does not yet know. It is being built in the open precisely because the kind of coordination it describes (across genuine difference, incorporating multiple perspectives, remaining humble about its own limitations) can only be developed through the practice of what it preaches. Certainty is not the goal. Adequacy under real conditions is.

---

These principles are the compass bearings. Work in the [frameworks](../03-frameworks/) directory derives from them. Future case studies will test them. Critiques defend or revise them.

The underlying conviction hasn't changed: coordination across genuine difference is possible, observable and currently under-theorised. The question is what formally grounds it. Being honest about incompleteness includes being honest about what that question hasn't yet answered.
