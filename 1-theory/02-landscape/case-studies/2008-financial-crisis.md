# The 2008 Global Financial Crisis

**Scale:** International
**Verdict:** Failure
**Referenced in:** [Where It Fails](../03-where-it-fails.md), [Why Now](../../01-core/02-why-now.md)

## Summary

The 2008 crisis exposed how fragile interconnected financial systems actually are. The collapse of the US mortgage market cascaded through global financial networks, triggering the worst economic downturn since the Great Depression.

## Why It Matters for the Framework

The canonical example of the accountability void as a coordination failure mode. Rating agencies rated the instruments. Banks originated the loans. Regulators oversaw the system. Investors bought the products. When the system collapsed, every actor could plausibly claim the failure was someone else's responsibility. Also demonstrates capture dynamics (Stigler's regulatory capture in action) and speed mismatch (regulatory frameworks designed for simpler financial instruments applied to complex derivatives).

## Key Patterns

- Accountability void: distributed responsibility producing zero accountability
- Capture: financial industry influence on regulatory design
- Speed mismatch: innovation outpacing regulation
- False consensus: widespread agreement that the system was stable, masking fragility
- Information distortion: risk models that obscured rather than revealed systemic exposure

## Open Questions

- Were the post-crisis reforms (Dodd-Frank, Basel III) adequate or cosmetic?
- Has the underlying coordination failure been addressed or merely papered over?
- What would a coordination framework that could have prevented this look like?
