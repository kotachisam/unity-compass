# Core

This is the intellectual foundation of the investigation. Five documents that make a single argument, best read in order.

## [Manifesto](01-MANIFESTO.md)

The stake in the ground. We have accepted the way we cooperate as if it were set in stone. It is not. Current models assume that coordination requires either a shared worldview or an immediate interest. Evidence from families, communities, open-source development, trilateral security arrangements and countless other domains shows otherwise. Coordination across genuine difference is possible, and treating convergence as a prerequisite leaves us stranded when it's unattainable. If value convergence is neither sufficient nor necessary for coordination, what actually grounds it?

## [Why Now](02-why-now.md)

The case for urgency. AI has introduced non-human actors into coordination for the first time. Several environmental and systemic tipping points have been crossed. The shortcuts that used to mask partial commensurability (ideological sorting, established hierarchies, the assumption that the people who mattered would eventually agree) are failing faster than they used to. The question isn't new, but the evasions are running out.

## [Why Not Already](03-why-not-already.md)

The diagnosis of stuckness. If the need is this urgent, why hasn't it been done? Five structural barriers that reinforce each other: ideas locked in academic institutions most practitioners never access, incentive structures that make rational actors behave in ways that prevent coordination, misalignment at the public-private intersection where coordination matters most, institutional path dependence, and the Overton window constraining what conversations can even be had.

## [Philosophical Foundation](04-philosophical-foundation.md)

Where coordination theory runs out, and what the formal toolkit leaves out. Game theory and mechanism design assume commensurable payoffs. Deliberative democracy assumes unlimited deliberation time. The philosophical inheritance underneath all of these has progressively narrowed what counts as rational: from Heraclitus' logos (capable of holding opposites in tension) to Aristotelian non-contradiction to Enlightenment formal inference. Five dimensions that formal coordination theory excludes are visible in successful coordination across difference: perspective-taking, tolerance for tension, situated wisdom, intellectual humility and relational trust. Whether these can be incorporated into a structural account of coordination is an open question.

## [Design Principles](05-design-principles.md)

Seven principles that commit the investigation to a specific shape: coordination without convergence; logos-breadth over logic-alone; pluralism-preserving; operationalisable; scale-aware (not yet scale-independent); barrier-aware; honest about incompleteness.

---

Together, these five documents argue that coordination theory is incomplete, the incompleteness is structural rather than accidental, and the principles for building something better can be derived from understanding what was excluded and why.
