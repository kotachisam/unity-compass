## 2026-04-11

**`2026-04-11T01:15+10:00` Tiered defensibility of structural features captured.** After the literature exploration on the structure/preferences distinction surfaced the Putnam concern, the user observed that the conditional retreat (Sen's positional objectivity) is too uniform across the four candidate structural features. Shared risk topology admits a stronger claim at the catastrophic-to-humanity level (nuclear war, runaway AI, climate collapse, pandemic, asteroid impact) because at that tier the categorisation is robustly shared across virtually any agent who values their own and their kind's continued existence. Captured as a new "Tiered defensibility of structural features" subsection in methodological-evolution.md under Formal object evolution. The tier structure: Tier 1 catastrophic (robust observer-independence with academic precedent in Bostrom and Ord), Tier 2 serious-but-not-existential (conditional version, AUKUS sits here), Tier 3 contested (out of scope). Asymmetric application across the four features: shared risk topology has a partial Tier 1 case, constraint topology has a partial Tier 1 case for physical constraints, entanglement density and procedural commitments need the conditional retreat throughout. New reading log section "Existential risk and the catastrophic-categorisation tier" added with Bostrom 2013 (P3, *Global Policy* article, the cleanest single citation), Ord 2020 (P3, *The Precipice*), Bostrom 2002 (P4, the founding paper). Open questions "Is structural fitness observer-independent?" updated with the tiered refinement. Uncommitted.
