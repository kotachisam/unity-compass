**Daniel Schmachtenberger** is an American systems thinker and social philosopher associated with the Consilience Project. His "metacrisis" framing is identified in the investigation as the closest existing work to its own diagnostic scope.

## The metacrisis framework

Schmachtenberger argues that individual crises (climate, AI, biosecurity, economic instability) share common generator functions: multipolar traps, coordination failures and sensemaking breakdowns. His concept of a "third attractor" (a path that avoids both catastrophe and authoritarian lockdown) parallels the investigation's concept of [[Coordination Without Convergence]].

## Role in the investigation

The investigation's current-approaches survey positions Schmachtenberger as the nearest comparator. Both projects identify the same problem space (coordination failure across existential domains) and both reject the assumption that existing institutional frameworks are adequate.

The investigation's critique: the metacrisis framework is stronger on diagnosis than prescription. It has generated extensive podcast and essay content but relatively little in the way of testable, operationalisable mechanisms. The EA Forum criticism is telling: the lack of written, interrogable framework makes it difficult to evaluate or build on.

The investigation distinguishes itself from Schmachtenberger on two points. First, the philosophical diagnosis: Schmachtenberger comes closest to the investigation's diagnostic instinct but does not trace coordination theory's limitations to their philosophical roots in the narrowing of [[Logos]]. Second, the specific formal question: the investigation asks a narrower, more formalisable question about [[Structural Interdependence]] as grounds for coordination.
