# Frameworks

This directory contains the analytical core of the investigation: the vocabulary, the empirical substrate, the formalism-boundary mapping, and the current working hypothesis the rest of the work builds on.

Five documents. The first three are the diagnosis (read in order). The last two are the proposal (evolving with the reading).

### The Diagnosis

**[Coordination Taxonomy](01-coordination-taxonomy.md)** maps five recurring strategic structures: pure coordination, social dilemmas, assurance games, chicken and coordination under value incommensurability. The last is the archetype existing theory handles worst and the one this investigation cares about most.

**[What Works and Why](02-what-works-and-why.md)** examines successful coordination without convergence in practice. Ostrom transfer analysis plus six worked cases produce a common architecture of five recurring elements.

**[Where Models Fail](03-where-models-fail.md)** maps the boundary of formal modelling across five traditions. Shows where formal tools work, work partially, and mislead.

### The Proposal

**[The Coordination Space (Draft)](04-the-coordination-space.md)** proposes a formal object: a space where value-alignment and structural-interdependence are axes in the same space, with stability defined by structural position rather than payoff. Post-first-reading scaffold with section markers for insights from Alexander's SEP entry. The pre-reading structural hypothesis is preserved as foundation; post-reading formal concepts (structural fitness, structural equilibrium, computational tractability, etiological application) are scaffolded for writing up.

**[The Formal Programme (Draft)](05-the-formal-programme.md)** captures the open questions and mathematical programme the reading surfaces. What math is needed, what distinguishes this from existing frameworks, what the remaining readings need to establish. A living document that grows with each source completed.
