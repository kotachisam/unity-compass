**Richard Bradley** is Professor of Philosophy at the London School of Economics and Political Science. His research focuses on decision theory, formal epistemology, social choice, scientific uncertainty and conditionals.

## Research focus

Bradley's *Decision Theory with a Human Face* (Cambridge UP, 2017) brings together his work on decision under ambiguity, belief revision and social choice. He has an ongoing collaboration with Kai Spiekermann (Professor of Political Philosophy, LSE Department of Government) through the Open Society Foundation on cohesion under severe uncertainty. This cross-departmental collaboration is directly adjacent to the investigation's central question.

### "Impartial Evaluation under Ambiguity" (2022)

Bradley's paper "Impartial Evaluation under Ambiguity" (*Ethics* 132(3), 2022) asks how an impartial but benevolent evaluator in the Harsanyi tradition should rank social prospects when standard expected utility calculation breaks down due to ambiguity. It was selected as one of the ten best philosophy articles of 2022 by the Philosopher's Annual.

Bradley works from three weak conditions on social betterness: **Outcome Anonymity** (indifference to reshuffling well-being across individuals), **State Dominance** (if X's outcome is at least as good as Y's in every state, X is at least as good overall) and **Pareto for Equivalent Outcomes** (a restricted unanimity condition on individual betterness). These assumptions are weaker than those imposed by mainstream rationality theories including Bayesian expected utility, Gilboa-Schmeidler maxmin, Klibanoff-Marinacci-Mukerji smooth ambiguity, rank-dependent utility and Buchak's risk-weighted utility.

Bradley proves three impossibility results. Section III shows that the conditions force homogeneity of individual betterness: all individuals must rank prospects identically. Section IV shows they force ambiguity neutrality: any sensitivity to ambiguity is precluded. Section V shows they force strong separability across states, recovering a version of the Sure-Thing Principle.

Section VI frames the situation as a trilemma: the theorist must reject ambiguity sensitivity, reject State Dominance or reject Pareto for Equivalent Outcomes. Bradley tentatively favours weakening Pareto, and in doing so flags [[Incomplete Preferences|incomplete betterness relations]] as unexplored territory, suggesting that "the rational response to lack of evidence or to disagreement among experts is partial suspension of judgment". He leaves the development of such relations, and their connection to [[Parity]], "for another day".

## Relevance to the investigation

Bradley is one of two target supervisors for the LSE MPhil/PhD application. His work is directly relevant to sub-question 3 of the research proposal: what does rational coordination require of agents who share [[Structural Interdependence]] but not values?

The investigation proposes that partial orderings over structural position-vectors are the formal analogue of Bradley's incomplete betterness relations, translated from well-being prospects to situated structural assessment. The thesis preserves structural analogues of State Dominance and Pareto (a configuration that is structurally better in every state is more stable; a configuration that improves every agent's structural position dominates) while replacing impartial evaluation with situated assessment from each agent's position in the coordination space. The working claim is that this move escapes the homogeneity and ambiguity-neutrality results, since those results are forced by impartiality (Outcome Anonymity) specifically rather than by the dominance and Pareto conditions. This is a working proposal, not a proven result: whether the structural reformulation genuinely escapes Bradley's impossibilities, or whether "structural position" covertly reintroduces prospect-ranking and inherits them, is an open question for the thesis. Bradley identified the door to incomplete betterness in Section VI; the thesis proposes to walk through it. See also [[Decision Theory Under Severe Uncertainty]].
