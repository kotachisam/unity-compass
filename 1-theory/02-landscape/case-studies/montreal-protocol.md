# The Montreal Protocol

**Scale:** International
**Verdict:** Success
**Referenced in:** [Where It Works](../02-where-it-works.md)

## Summary

The 1987 Montreal Protocol coordinated 197 nations to phase out ozone-depleting substances. Widely regarded as the most successful international environmental agreement in history.

## Why It Matters for the Investigation

The strongest available evidence that coordination across genuine difference at planetary scale is possible. Nations with radically different economic systems, political ideologies and development priorities coordinated on a shared existential threat without requiring ideological convergence.

## Key Patterns

- Shared stakes without shared ideology: ozone depletion threatened everyone regardless of political system
- Iterative trust-building: phased implementation with built-in review periods
- Clear mechanism: specific substances, measurable targets, transparent monitoring
- Financial mechanism (Multilateral Fund) addressed asymmetric costs between developed and developing nations

## Open Questions

- Why did this work when climate coordination hasn't? What conditions were present here that are absent for climate?
- Was the relatively contained scope (specific chemicals, identifiable manufacturers) a necessary condition or an incidental one?
- Does the success generalise, or was it a unique alignment of circumstances?
