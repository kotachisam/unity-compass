# Design Principles

The previous documents established two things. [Where coordination theory runs out](04-philosophical-foundation.md): when actors hold genuinely different values and the standard toolkit of negotiation, aggregation and compromise can't bridge the gap. And what's actually missing: capacities like empathy, tolerance for sustained tension, situated wisdom, intellectual humility and relational trust — all excluded from formal coordination theory by its philosophical inheritance, disciplinary origins and methodological constraints.

These design principles are the bridge between that diagnosis and the [frameworks](../03-frameworks/) that follow. Each one responds to a specific dimension of the problem and is shaped by the [barriers to implementation](03-why-not-already.md) that any real-world coordination mechanism has to survive.

---

**1. Coordination without convergence.**
The framework must enable collective action between actors who hold genuinely different values, worldviews and goals. It must never require ideological agreement as a precondition for cooperation. Where convergence emerges, it should be a byproduct of coordination, not a prerequisite for it.

**2. Logos-breadth over logic-alone.**
The framework must incorporate dimensions of practical reason that formal logic excludes: situated knowledge, embodied wisdom, tolerance for contradiction, relational intelligence. It draws on the full bandwidth of human reasoning capacity rather than restricting itself to formal inference. This means expanding what rigour includes rather than relegating anything outside it to the back-bench.

**3. Pluralism-preserving.**
The framework must protect genuine difference rather than eroding it. Coordination that works by gradually homogenising its participants has succeeded at cooperation but failed at pluralism. The goal is coordination that recognises diversity as the structural asset it is, rather than seeing it as an obstacle to be overcome.

**4. Operationalisable.**
The framework must produce mechanisms that actual institutions can test, implement and evaluate. Principles that cannot be translated into practice — however philosophically elegant — fail the test this project sets for itself. Every theoretical claim must connect to a concrete coordination mechanism with identifiable success criteria.

**5. Scale-independent.**
The same coordination patterns appear at every scale: between individuals, within teams, across organisations, between nations. The framework must be coherent across these scales. A principle that works for family disagreements but breaks at the level of international coordination (or vice versa) is incomplete.

**6. Barrier-aware.**
The framework must be designed with the [barriers to implementation](03-why-not-already.md) explicitly in mind. Academically rigorous enough to survive peer review. Practically grounded enough to function inside broken incentive structures. Accessible enough to escape the rooms where coordination theory has been trapped. If a principle cannot survive contact with the institutional realities documented in this repository, it needs revision.

**7. Honest about incompleteness.**
The framework acknowledges what it does not yet know. It is being built in the open precisely because the kind of coordination it describes — across genuine difference, incorporating multiple perspectives, remaining humble about its own limitations — can only be developed through the practice of what it preaches. Certainty is not the goal. Adequacy under real conditions is.

---

These principles are the compass bearings. Everything in the [frameworks](../03-frameworks/) directory derives from them. Everything in the [case studies](../../2-practice/03-case-studies/) tests them. And everything in the [critiques and responses](../../3-project/critiques-and-responses/) defends or revises them. The frameworks that follow are where these principles meet the reality of what we know and what we don't yet know.

The conviction underneath all of it remains what it was at the start: that the human capacities required for coordination across genuine difference — empathy, humility, the ability to hold tension, the wisdom that lives in bodies and relationships rather than abstractions - are the unavoidable engineering specifications required for a species that wants to survive what's coming.

We are all built from the same materials. The same atoms, the same stardust, the same brief tenancy on the same fragile planet. If that shared ground can serve as a foundation for coordination, it would be a waste not to build on it. And the only question is whether we will do so in time.
