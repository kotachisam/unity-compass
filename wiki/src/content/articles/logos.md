**Logos** is an ancient Greek term that originally meant something far richer than what is now called logic. For [[Heraclitus]], it was a cosmic ordering principle that included the capacity to hold opposites in tension without forcing resolution. The investigation identifies the progressive narrowing of logos to formal logic as the primary point of observation connecting the multiple factors that make coordination theory incomplete.

## The narrowing

Over the course of Western philosophy, logos was progressively narrowed:

1. **Heraclitus** (c. 535-475 BCE): logos as a principle of unity-in-opposition, the capacity to hold contraries together. Fragment 10: "Thou shouldst unite things whole and things not whole, that which tends to unite and that which tends to separate, the harmonious and the discordant."

2. **Plato**: privileged permanence over flux, shifting emphasis from Heraclitean tension-holding to stable forms.

3. **Aristotle**: formalised non-contradiction as an absolute constraint, excluding the possibility of productive contradiction from what counts as rational.

4. **The Enlightenment**: logic became synonymous with formal inference from clear premises. The capacity for holding tension, for pattern recognition that precedes deduction, for wisdom embedded in situation rather than abstraction, was excluded from what "reason" meant.

## Significance for the investigation

The investigation argues that this narrowing matters because everything downstream inherits it. If the foundational framework for practical reason says that contradiction is impermissible and that knowledge requires abstraction from the particular, then every discipline built on those foundations will carry the same constraints. [[Game Theory]], [[Mechanism Design]], [[Social Choice Theory]] and classical decision theory all operate within the narrowed framework.

This is not the only factor making coordination theory incomplete. The investigation identifies multiple converging factors: disciplinary constraints, adversarial origins at RAND, the tractability trap in academic incentives, cultural narrowness. But the narrowing of logos runs through all of them as a philosophical inheritance they share.

## Design principle: logos-breadth over logic-alone

The investigation's design principles include "logos-breadth over logic-alone": keeping visible the dimensions of practical reason that formal logic excludes (situated knowledge, embodied wisdom, tolerance for contradiction, relational intelligence). Whether these dimensions can be incorporated into a structural account or whether they remain permanent residue that formal theory cannot capture is an open question.
