# The Unity Compass

**Coordination frameworks for a world that can't agree but can't afford not to.**

> *Community nickname: the PISS (Post-Ideological Society System) — because we're done with ideological pissing contests.*

**Status:** v0.1 Pre-alpha | **Started:** December 2025

---

## The Problem

We're facing planet-scale threats with institutions built for a different era. Climate change, AI, pandemics, economic fragility: none of these respect national borders, party lines or election cycles. And yet every serious attempt at coordination collapses into the same two failure modes. Either we try to force everyone to agree first (which never happens in time) or we negotiate endlessly while the window closes.

Left vs right. Capitalism vs socialism. National sovereignty vs global governance. These frameworks were built for industrial-age problems. They're not *wrong*, but they're not enough. The coordination challenges ahead of us move faster, cut deeper and demand cooperation between people who will never share the same worldview.

So the question becomes: can we coordinate without requiring everyone to agree?

## The Approach

The Unity Compass works like its namesake. A compass gives you shared direction without dictating your route. Everyone can use the same compass while travelling different paths. It's a tool, not a destination.

Rather than proposing yet another ideology, this framework steps back and asks what actually enables large-scale cooperation when people hold genuinely different values. It draws on philosophy, systems thinking, game theory, institutional design and real-world case studies to develop coordination principles that are rigorous enough to survive academic scrutiny, specific enough for institutions to test and clear enough for anyone to engage with.

Three questions drive the work:

1. When the stakes are existential but people disagree on almost everything else, what makes coordination possible?
2. What kinds of institutions can enable cooperation without demanding that everyone think the same way, while still avoiding the usual traps of corruption, short-termism and bureaucratic paralysis?
3. As AI systems become participants in governance and markets (not just tools), how do we keep meaningful human agency in the loop?

This repository has two content spines and a project infrastructure layer.

**Theory** is the philosophical argument: what the framework claims and why. Start here if you want the intellectual foundations.

**Project** is everything that serves the whole: community, audience-specific entry points, responses to critiques and resources.

## Why Two Spines?

Most frameworks just give you the argument. Here's the theory, here are the conclusions, off you go. The Unity Compass does that too. But every tradition that has actually succeeded at enabling cooperation between people who disagree, from commons governance to open-source software to contemplative practice, shares a common insight: understanding the idea isn't enough. You have to *practise* it.

The theory spine makes the case. The practice spine makes it real. The case studies are where the two meet: rigorous analysis alongside the lived experience of what coordination across genuine difference actually feels like from the inside.
