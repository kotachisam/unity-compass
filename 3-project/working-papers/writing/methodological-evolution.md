# Methodological Evolution

How the investigation's formal positioning evolves as reading progresses. Updated after every reading. Not a tensions tracker (those were V1→V2 transition artifacts). This is the ongoing record of where the thesis sits relative to key thinkers and frameworks, and how each reading shifts that positioning.

## Supervisor positioning

### J. McKenzie Alexander (constructive tools)

Provides the formal apparatus the thesis extends: evolutionary dynamics, spatial topology, population-level norm evolution. The thesis positions as complementary to his programme: Alexander explains how coordination norms emerge over populations over time; the thesis asks what grounds coordination in the specific case, before evolutionary dynamics have operated. His call for "an alternate theory of utility/fitness compatible with bounded rationality" (SEP 2021, section 6.1) is the formal opening the thesis walks through, proposing structural position as that fitness measure.

**Status after reading Alexander SEP (2026-04-06):** alignment confirmed. Complementary not competing. Need to articulate what "structural interdependence" captures that his "population structure" doesn't. Differentiator: procedural commitments and shared risk as formal variables, plus the acute-case framing.

**Status during Alexander OSE reading (2026-04-09):** three developments. (1) The complementarity framing may need revision. Alexander doesn't deploy EGT in this book at all. The thesis may be the BRIDGE between his two disconnected bodies of work (formal EGT and diagnostic Open Society) rather than complementary to one of them. (2) Alexander's Open Society concept lacks an account of its own implementation conditions. The coordination space provides what his concept is missing: a formal specification of what structural configurations make a given arrangement sustainable. This is a stronger positioning than "complementary to his EGT work." (3) Gaus (2021) formalised fitness landscapes for open societies using complex systems theory. Alexander explicitly distances himself from Gaus. The thesis must articulate how it differs from Gaus's formalism. Added as P1 reading.

### Richard Bradley (critical pressure)

Maps the constraints the thesis escapes. His paper proves that even weak assumptions (Outcome Anonymity, State Dominance, Pareto for Equivalent Outcomes) force homogeneity, ambiguity-neutrality, and strong separability. The thesis preserves State Dominance and Pareto while replacing impartial evaluation with situated structural assessment, escaping the homogeneity and ambiguity-neutrality results.

Bradley doesn't need to agree with the thesis. He needs to understand the territory well enough to test whether the escape actually works. A supervisor who built the room is more valuable for testing whether you've genuinely left it.

**Status after reading Bradley S1-4 (2026-04-07):** the rival models Bradley references (cumulative prospect theory, rank-dependent utility, risk-weighted expected utility) all violate the Sure-Thing Principle, allowing context-dependence between states. Real coordination violates strong separability as a feature (AUKUS Pillar I stability depends on Pillar II). These rival models may serve the thesis's formal needs better than Bradley's own framework, but Bradley's diagnostic mapping of the constraints remains essential for positioning.

### Ruth Chang (foundational concept, not supervisor)

Provides the philosophical concept (parity/incommensurability) the thesis both relies on and extends. Chang defines parity for evaluative comparisons between options: comparable but not rankable on a single scale. The thesis extends parity to structural positions in the coordination space: two configurations are comparable (measurable on structural axes) but neither dominates (each better on different axes). This is a substantive claim about Chang's concept applied to a new domain.

**Open question:** does Chang think parity is extendable to structural domains? Or would she resist the move on grounds that parity is specifically about evaluative comparisons between options, not about structural configurations? The thesis needs to argue for the extension, not assume it. The argument: structural positions ARE comparable (both assessable on objective structural grounds) and can exhibit non-dominance (better on different axes), which satisfies Chang's conditions for parity. Whether she'd accept this needs to be tested, ideally by reading her response to structural interpretations of parity in the existing literature.

**Status:** Chang previously expressed interest in a supervision relationship (Oxford, V1 proposal era). The investigation has moved to LSE targeting Alexander and Bradley. Chang remains a key theorist whose framework the thesis builds on. Potential academic correspondent as the work develops.

## Formal object evolution

### Structural equilibrium

Originally framed as "not Nash." Refined to: Nash generalised to partial orderings over structural position-vectors. The formal novelty is the space (configuration space, not strategy space) and the mathematical object (partial ordering, not cardinal utility), not the equilibrium concept (no-unilateral-deviation inherited from Nash). The thesis question: does this weaker object produce results the stronger one can't?

### Structural axes

Candidate features (risk exposure, entanglement density, constraint burden, procedural commitment strength) are empirically motivated from coordination-without-convergence cases, not axiomatically derived. The set is open. The formal programme tests sufficiency and necessity. The axes are not claimed as exhaustive.

### Parity in the structural domain

Two configurations exhibit parity when each agent can assess both on structural grounds (objective, measurable) but neither dominates (better on different axes). Subjectivity enters in weighting, not measurement. Parity emerges from the interaction between objective structure and subjective weighting. Grounds Chang's concept without requiring Bradley's impartial evaluator.

### Objective vs subjective structure (from Tension 6)

Structural features are objective properties of a configuration. How agents perceive and weight them is perspective-dependent. Layer 1 (objective) is where stability is defined. Layer 2 (subjective) is where coordination behaviour comes from. The thesis contribution is Layer 1. The divergence between layers is itself informative.

## Key formal claims (current state)

**Core claim (what the thesis argues):**
1. The coordination space preserves State Dominance and Pareto while replacing impartial evaluation with situated structural assessment, thereby escaping the restrictive results Bradley (2022) shows are forced by impartiality

**Formal mechanism (how it works):**
2. Structural equilibrium is Nash generalised to partial orderings over position-vectors in a configuration space

**Supporting architecture (what the thesis uses):**
3. The structural axes are empirically motivated and open, not axiomatically closed
4. Parity in the structural domain grounds Chang's concept through the interaction of objective structure and subjective weighting
5. Computational tractability: structural state space may be lower-dimensional than strategy space
6. Etiological application: trajectories through the coordination space constrain how coordination phenomena emerge

The proposal leads with 1, defends via 2, gestures at 3-6. A successful thesis establishes 1 and 2 thoroughly and demonstrates 3-6 in one applied domain (AUKUS). Claims 3-6 are the research programme beyond the thesis.
