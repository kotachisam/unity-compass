# Impartial Evaluation under Ambiguity

Bradley's most directly relevant single paper for the investigation. Evaluates social prospects from an impartial standpoint when standard expected utility calculation breaks down due to ambiguity.

Selected as one of the ten best philosophy articles of 2022 by the Philosopher's Annual.

## Why it matters

The investigation's key question for Bradley's framework: this paper evaluates from an external, impartial standpoint. Coordination requires agents to act from within their own partial perspectives. The gap between impartial evaluation and situated coordination is where the thesis operates. Can Bradley's framework be extended from impartial evaluation to situated, first-person coordination?

## Notes

### On section 1: the impartiality assumption

The "impartial but benevolent rational evaluator" is another view-from-nowhere. Useful fiction for mathematical tractability, same way the common payoff matrix is a useful fiction for game theory. There is no such thing as absolute impartiality, only degrees of leaning into benevolence or maliciousness. This is the partial-commensurability move applied to the evaluator: not impartial vs partial, but graduated partiality as a variable.

**Key question for the thesis:** does Bradley treat impartiality as a simplifying assumption he's aware of (what happens when you relax it?) or as a foundational requirement (framework breaks without it)? If the latter, the gap between his framework and the coordination space is precisely characterisable: what formal properties survive when you replace the impartial evaluator with situated agents at specific positions in the coordination space?

→ connects to [[Richard Bradley]], [[Value Incommensurability]], [[Logos]], [[Structural Interdependence]]

### On section 1 continued: Bradley's dilemma and the alignment

Bradley's surprising result: even with assumptions weaker than ANY mainstream rationality theory (Bayesian, Gilboa-Schmeidler maxmin, Klibanoff-Marinacci-Mukerji smooth ambiguity, Quiggin rank-dependent, Tversky-Kahneman prospect theory, Buchak risk-weighted), the weak constraints are STILL sufficient to rule out: (a) heterogeneity in how individuals evaluate prospects, (b) sensitivity to ambiguity, (c) violation of the Sure-Thing Principle.

This creates his dilemma: accept these restrictive implications, or abandon either weak rationality requirements or benevolent sensitivity to individual well-being.

**The alignment sentence:** "In principle, this response opens up a space for impartial social evaluation that is not expectational in form and which can exhibit forms of sensitivity to uncertainty disallowed by Harsanyi's theory." This is doing the same structural move as the thesis: opening formal space beyond the standard framework by weakening assumptions. The question is whether the coordination space occupies a DIFFERENT opened space (structural position, no outcome ranking) or the SAME opened space (weakened evaluation, still ranking outcomes).

**The thesis question sharpens:** Bradley finds that even weak assumptions force strong constraints within the evaluation-of-prospects framework. Does the coordination space escape those constraints by being a different formal object entirely (characterising stability rather than ranking prospects)? Or does "structural position" secretly reintroduce prospect-ranking and inherit Bradley's impossibility?

### On section 3: homogeneity of betterness

Bradley proves that his three conditions force all individual betterness relations to be identical. He acknowledges agents reasonably assign different probabilities under uncertainty but rejects this interpretation because it's "inconsistent with rational Paretian social evaluation."

The framework dictates what reality is allowed to look like rather than adapting to it. Formal coherence chosen over descriptive adequacy. The same logos-narrowing move: discard situated embodiment to preserve the formal apparatus.

**For the thesis:** Bradley (2022) demonstrates that even weak rationality assumptions force homogeneity of individual betterness. This motivates the search for frameworks that accommodate the heterogeneous evaluations characterising real coordination under partial commensurability. The coordination space operates in the territory Bradley explicitly discards.

### On section 4: ambiguity and the parallel to "neither sufficient nor necessary"

Bradley identifies that ambiguity aversion is "rationally permitted, though not required" (via Ellsberg 1961). Agents can legitimately differ in their response to ambiguity without either being irrational. This is structurally parallel to the thesis's "neither sufficient nor necessary": a space where reason underdetermines choice, where rational agents can occupy different positions. Chang's parity operates in the same territory.

Bradley then proves his framework collapses that space: Paretian social evaluation precludes any sensitivity to ambiguity. The openness gets forced shut by the assumptions.

**The value of the paper for the thesis:** Bradley maps the walls of the room. He proves which assumptions force which constraints, and frames the result as a trilemma (section VI): reject ambiguity sensitivity, reject State Dominance, or reject Pareto for Equivalent Outcomes. He doesn't prescribe which wall to break. The thesis proposes to leave the room entirely: a coordination space that preserves the rational space Bradley's framework collapses.

**Supervision framing:** Bradley has precisely mapped the constraints that the thesis needs to escape. His diagnostic rigour (showing exactly where and why the framework forces restrictive results) is the foundation the thesis builds on. The outsider perspective (structural position replacing well-being evaluation) complements his insider mapping of the formal limits.

**Formal relationship to the coordination space:** Bradley's three walls are Outcome Anonymity, State Dominance, and Pareto for Equivalent Outcomes. State Dominance and Pareto translate to the coordination space (structurally better in every state → more stable; everyone's structural position improves → more stable). Impartiality drops out because coordination stability is assessed from each agent's situated position, not from an impartial standpoint. Rationality weakens because "structural reason to stay" is observable (risk exposure lower inside than outside) rather than requiring preference orderings over infinite lotteries.

The coordination space preserves the structural analogues of State Dominance and Pareto while replacing impartial evaluation with situated structural assessment, thereby escaping the homogeneity and ambiguity-neutrality results that Bradley (2022) shows are forced by impartiality.

**Formal precision on the structural equilibrium (refined from Alexander notes):** structural equilibrium is Nash generalised to partial orderings over position-vectors. The formal novelty is the space and the weaker mathematical object (partial ordering, not cardinal utility), not the equilibrium concept itself (no-unilateral-deviation is inherited). The thesis question is whether the weaker object produces results the stronger one can't.

**On the structural axes:** candidate features (risk exposure, entanglement density, constraint burden, procedural commitment strength) are empirically motivated from observed coordination-without-convergence cases, not axiomatically derived. The set is open. The formal programme tests sufficiency and necessity of each. Additional axes accommodate as the empirical base expands.

**Parity in the structural domain (connecting Chang to Bradley):** two configurations exhibit parity when each agent can assess both on structural grounds (objective, measurable) but neither dominates (each better on different axes). Subjectivity enters in the weighting, not the measurement. Parity emerges from the interaction between objective structure and subjective weighting. This grounds Chang's concept without requiring it to be a property of either alone, and without requiring Bradley's impartial evaluator.

### On sections 5-6: separability, the trilemma, and incomplete betterness

Section V: framework forces strong separability. Real coordination violates this (AUKUS Pillar I depends on Pillar II). Rival models (cumulative prospect theory, rank-dependent utility, risk-weighted expected utility) allow this violation.

Section VI trilemma: reject (1) ambiguity/risk sensitivity, (2) State Dominance, or (3) Pareto for Equivalent Outcomes. Bradley identifies (3) as most promising. The thesis leaves the space where the trilemma is defined: it doesn't rank prospects, so the trilemma dissolves.

**Key passage:** Bradley flags incomplete betterness relations as unexplored territory ("left for another day"): "the rational response to lack of evidence or to disagreement among experts is partial suspension of judgment." Incomplete betterness is exactly where partial orderings over structural position-vectors operate. The rational response to incommensurability is not forced ranking but acknowledged non-dominance. This IS the thesis's territory. Bradley identified the door but didn't walk through it.

1. Incomplete betterness relations under ambiguity (the thesis's direct territory)
2. Whether dropping the Equality clause opens space for non-standard uncertainty attitudes
3. What form social betterness should take beyond blocking Harsanyi's Utilitarianism
4. What strengthening of Paretian unanimity is justified to decide between alternative theories

Door 1 isn't adjacent to the thesis. It IS the thesis, translated from well-being evaluation to structural assessment. The thesis answers a structural analogue of Bradley's open question: incomplete orderings where the rational response to incommensurability is acknowledged non-dominance across structural position-vectors rather than forced ranking of well-being prospects. The novelty is in the structural character of the ordering (position-vectors rather than well-being), not in the incompleteness itself. ## Takeaways

Bradley proves that three weak conditions (Outcome Anonymity, State Dominance, Pareto for Equivalent Outcomes) force restrictive results: homogeneity of individual betterness (section III), ambiguity neutrality (section IV), and strong separability/Sure-Thing Principle (section V). Even assumptions weaker than any mainstream rationality theory produce these constraints. This creates a trilemma (section VI): reject ambiguity sensitivity, State Dominance, or Pareto for Equivalent Outcomes.

The thesis's relationship to Bradley is precise: it preserves the structural analogues of State Dominance and Pareto while replacing impartial evaluation with situated structural assessment. This escapes the homogeneity and ambiguity-neutrality results because those are forced by impartiality specifically. The coordination space doesn't rank prospects by well-being; it characterises structural configurations. Bradley's trilemma dissolves because the thesis operates in a different formal space.

The most significant finding for the thesis is Bradley's identification of incomplete betterness relations as unexplored territory: "the rational response to lack of evidence is partial suspension of judgment." Partial orderings over structural position-vectors ARE incomplete betterness relations. Bradley identified the door but didn't walk through it. The thesis walks through it into a coordination space where non-dominance across structural axes (Chang's parity applied to structural positions) replaces forced ranking.

### On section 2: the formal framework

Bradley's setup: social prospects are distributions of well-being over individuals and states. A table where rows = individuals, columns = states, cells = well-being magnitudes. The evaluator ranks these with a betterness ordering (≿).

Three key assumptions he makes explicit:
1. **No assumption about kind of uncertainty.** Results apply to ambiguity, objective probability, and subjective probability alike.
2. **Well-being is interpersonally comparable and numerically measurable.** He acknowledges this is nontrivial but assumes methods exist.
3. **The evaluator is not omniscient.** Potentially imperfect information about moral and empirical facts.

The risk/uncertainty neutrality distinction: an evaluator is neutral if indifferent between (5 for sure) and (10 at p=0.5, 0 at p=0.5). Averse if they prefer less spread. Expected utility doesn't require a particular attitude but restricts the patterns of attitudes.

**For the thesis:** assumption 2 is where Bradley's framework and the coordination space diverge most sharply. He assumes well-being is numerically measurable and interpersonally comparable. The entire coordination-space proposal exists because this assumption fails under value incommensurability. If V for agent 1 and V for agent 2 are different kinds of value (Chang's parity), the well-being magnitudes in Bradley's table cells can't be filled in. His framework literally cannot represent the problem the thesis addresses.

But the betterness ordering (≿) over social prospects is interesting. Could structural position be ordered (≿) without requiring numerical well-being? "Configuration A is structurally better than configuration B for agent i" might be definable in terms of risk exposure, entanglement robustness, etc., without requiring that the well-being cells have numbers in them.

**Reference:** Adler, M. *Measuring Social Welfare: An Introduction* (Oxford UP, 2019) - Bradley's footnote 9, cited for methods of measuring and comparing individual well-being. Worth checking for whether any measurement approaches work without full cardinal comparability. [SSRN preprint](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3541745), [OUP page](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/measuring-social-welfare-9780190643027). Adler is at Duke (Law + Economics + Philosophy + Public Policy).

**Formal intuition:** structural betterness ordering over position-vectors (r, e, c = risk exposure, entanglement robustness, constraint burden) as a multi-dimensional partial ordering. Stability = no unilateral move improves all structural axes simultaneously (structural Pareto condition). This gives Chang's parity a formal representation: two configurations are "on a par" when they're ordered differently on different structural axes with no dominance relation. The coordination space quantifies parity rather than dissolving it.

### On section 2: the formal framework in detail

Bradley's betterness relation requires: completeness, transitivity, continuity, monotonicity in well-being. Three key assumptions on social betterness:

1. **Outcome Anonymity** (impartiality): indifference between any reshuffling of well-being across individuals. Presupposes well-being doesn't depend on who enjoys it.
2. **State Dominance**: if X's outcome is at least as good as Y's in every state, X is at least as good overall. Implies weak separability across states.
3. **Strong Pareto**: social betterness supervenes positively on individual betterness. Three interpretations: subjective (preference), objective (actual goodness), judgmental (evaluator's assessment). Bradley adopts the judgmental interpretation.

**On "well-informed and of clear mind":** Bradley treats this as a throwaway epistemic condition on preferences. But being well-informed and of clear mind are in fact rarities, not defaults. Real coordination agents operate under cognitive load, emotional pressure, institutional constraints, incomplete information, and the situated biases the [[Logos]] argument identifies. The entire EGT literature (Alexander's section 4.2 on hyperrational agents) exists because this condition almost never holds. Building it into even a "weak" framework smuggles in an assumption that excludes most real coordination situations.

**Structural parallel:** Bradley's three assumptions (anonymity, state dominance, strong Pareto) all operate on well-being distributions. For the coordination space:
- Anonymity → structural position doesn't depend on which agent occupies it (the topology matters, not the labels)
- State dominance → if configuration A has better structural position for all agents in all states, A is more stable
- Pareto → coordination stability supervenes on individual structural positions

These translations might preserve Bradley's formal machinery while replacing well-being with structural position. The question is whether his impossibility results carry over or whether the structural version escapes them.

**Reference:** Broome, J. *Weighing Goods: Equality, Uncertainty and Time* (Oxford UP, 1991) - cited for the Principle of Personal Good (objective interpretation of Pareto).
