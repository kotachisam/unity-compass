# Philosophical Tensions: V2 Research Proposal × Unity Compass Repository

**Status:** Working paper | **Date:** April 2026
**Purpose:** Track genuine philosophical tensions between the doctoral research proposal (V2.0) and the broader Unity Compass framework. These are not inconsistencies to be resolved editorially but open questions the research programme should address.

---

## Tension 1: Structural Grounding vs. Agent Capacities

**Where it appears:** The v0.1 philosophical foundation (01-core/04-philosophical-foundation.md) argued that coordination across genuine difference "mechanically requires" five capacities: empathy/perspective-taking, holding tension without premature resolution, situated wisdom, intellectual humility and relational trust. The V2 research proposal hypothesises that structural features of interdependence (shared risk topology, shared constraints, network entanglement, procedural commitments) can ground coordination without requiring shared values or aligned intentions.

**The conflict:** If the philosophical foundation is right that coordination *requires* these agent capacities, then structural features alone are not sufficient and V2's central hypothesis is false. If V2 is right that structural interdependence can ground coordination independently, then the philosophical foundation overclaims what coordination requires.

**Why it matters:** This is the deepest tension in the project. The resolution is unlikely to be a clean victory for either side.

**Candidate resolutions:**
- The capacities are how agents *recognise and respond to* structural grounds for coordination rather than what *constitutes* those grounds. Empathy might be how agents identify shared risk topology. Humility might be what allows agents to acknowledge network entanglement. The capacities are epistemic access conditions rather than constitutive grounds.
- The capacities are enabling conditions that make structural coordination more robust or more likely to emerge, without being strictly necessary. Structural interdependence can ground coordination without them, but coordination is fragile or unstable without the capacities to sustain it.
- The structural and capacity accounts operate at different levels of analysis. Structural features ground coordination *formally*. Capacities explain why it *succeeds or fails operationally*. Both are true; they answer different questions.

**Resolution pathway:** Reframe the philosophical foundation (Option 1 from the original diagnosis): present the capacities as territory the investigation needs to navigate rather than prerequisites it must satisfy. Let the formal investigation determine the relationship between structural grounds and agent capacities rather than presupposing the answer.

## Tension 2: Formalisability vs. the Logos-Breadth Claim

**Where it appears:** The On Logos essay argues that the narrowing of logos to logic excluded essential dimensions of practical reason: situated knowledge, embodied wisdom, tolerance for contradiction, relational intelligence. The V2 research proposal commits to formal characterisation results using game-theoretic and decision-theoretic modelling.

**The conflict:** If the formal toolkit is part of the problem (as On Logos argues), why is a formal result the solution (as V2 proposes)? A critic could read the two documents together and conclude either that V2 contradicts On Logos or that V2 is unwittingly reproducing the narrowing it claims to diagnose.

**Why it matters:** This tension is more apparent than real, but it requires explicit articulation to avoid the appearance of self-contradiction.

**Candidate resolution:** V2 is not abandoning formalism. It is expanding what the formal toolkit is *permitted to represent*. The narrowing On Logos identifies is not a problem with formal methods per se but with the restriction of formal methods to a particular class of inputs (quantifiable preferences, constructible payoff structures). V2 proposes to formalise structural features that the existing toolkit excludes by design: partial orderings over catastrophic outcomes, constraint structures, network entanglement. The formal programme widens the inputs to the formal apparatus rather than rejecting the apparatus itself.

The analogy: non-Euclidean geometry didn't abandon rigour. It abandoned an axiom (the parallel postulate) and discovered that rigorous mathematics could operate on broader foundations. V2 is attempting something structurally similar for coordination theory.

**What this means for the thesis:** At some point the thesis needs a methodological section that explicitly addresses this. Something along the lines of: "The logos-breadth recovery motivates asking which dimensions formal coordination theory has excluded. The formal programme then asks whether those dimensions can be incorporated into the formal apparatus rather than remaining outside it. The critique of narrowing and the formal programme are complementary, not contradictory."

## Tension 3: Scale Independence vs. Doctoral Tractability

**Where it appears:** The Unity Compass repository claims that the same coordination patterns operate at every scale from interpersonal to civilisational (design principle 5). The V2 research proposal correctly narrows to multilateral defence coordination with a robustness check against one other domain.

**The conflict:** The repo makes a universal claim. The thesis tests it at one scale. If the formal results only hold for multilateral defence coordination, the scale-independence claim is unsupported. If the thesis claims scale-independence without testing it across scales, the claim is unearned.

**Why it matters:** Scale independence is one of the Unity Compass's most distinctive and most ambitious claims. It cannot be fully validated within a single doctoral thesis, but it cannot be left entirely unaddressed either.

**Candidate resolution:** The thesis needs a principled account of *why* structural interdependence should generalise across scales without being required to *demonstrate* that it does across all of them. Possible approaches:
- Argue that the structural features identified (shared risk topology, constraints, network entanglement, procedural commitments) are scale-neutral by construction: they describe *relationships between agents* rather than properties of agents, and relationships have the same formal structure regardless of whether the agents are individuals, institutions or nations.
- Use the robustness check domain (climate governance, AI regulation) not just as a confirmation exercise but as a *scale contrast*: if the formal results hold in a domain with different scale characteristics, the claim to scale independence gains empirical traction.
- Explicitly flag scale independence as a conjecture the thesis motivates but does not prove, and identify what further work would be needed to establish it. Honest boundary-drawing is more credible than overreach.

## Tension 4: Convergence as Non-Necessary vs. Convergence as Emergent

**Where it appears:** The Unity Compass manifesto states that where convergence emerges, it should be "a byproduct of coordination, not a prerequisite for it." The V2 research proposal argues that convergence is neither sufficient nor necessary for coordination.

**The conflict:** These claims are compatible but subtly different in their implications. The manifesto implies convergence *will* emerge from successful coordination (it's a byproduct). V2 is agnostic about whether convergence emerges at all (it's simply not necessary). The manifesto makes a predictive claim about the *consequences* of coordination. V2 makes a structural claim about its *grounds*.

**Why it matters:** If structurally grounded coordination *produces* convergence over time, that's a strong empirical prediction and a potential selling point for the framework. If it merely *bypasses* the need for convergence, that's the safer philosophical position but it makes the framework less distinctive: lots of approaches can work around value disagreement in the short term.

**Candidate resolution:** This may be an empirical question that the formal apparatus can illuminate. Possible formal results:
- Structural coordination is stable *without* convergence emerging: agents continue to hold incommensurable values but coordinate successfully through structural entanglement indefinitely. This would vindicate V2's agnosticism and require revising the manifesto's "byproduct" language.
- Structural coordination tends to *produce* partial convergence over time through iterated interaction, even when it doesn't require it as an input. This would vindicate both documents: convergence is not a prerequisite but it is a predictable consequence. This would also connect to Alexander's evolutionary dynamics work.
- The relationship is domain-dependent: structural coordination produces convergence in some domains (where iterated interaction occurs) and not others (where interaction is episodic or non-repeating). This would be the most nuanced and potentially most interesting result.

**What this means for the thesis:** This tension is probably best left as an open question that the formal investigation addresses rather than resolved in advance. The thesis could include a section examining whether structural coordination equilibria are convergence-generating or convergence-neutral under different conditions.

## Tension 5: The Boundary Problem

**Where it appears:** The V2 research proposal commits to identifying "the degree of structural entanglement below which interdependence cannot ground coordination, and the conditions, if any, under which incommensurability is sufficiently total that no structural feature provides purchase."

**The tension:** This is not a tension between documents but the hardest open question in the thesis. What distinguishes navigable from non-navigable incommensurability?

**Why it matters:** The boundary conditions determine the scope and significance of the entire framework. If structural interdependence can ground coordination under *all* conditions of incommensurability, the framework is more powerful than expected but the claim will be hard to defend. If there are clear conditions under which it fails, the framework is bounded but honest, and the boundary itself becomes philosophically informative.

**Candidate variables:**
- *Degree of incommensurability:* Is there a threshold of value divergence beyond which structural features cannot generate stable coordination? What determines this threshold?
- *Type of structural entanglement:* Are some structural features more robust to deep incommensurability than others? Shared risk topology (agreement on what to avoid) might survive conditions that network entanglement (mere interconnection) cannot.
- *Interaction between degree and type:* The boundary may not be a simple threshold but a function of the relationship between the depth of incommensurability and the strength of structural entanglement. Deep incommensurability may be navigable when structural entanglement is sufficiently strong, and shallow incommensurability may be un-navigable when structural features are weak.
- *Temporal dimension:* Coordination may be achievable in the short term under deep incommensurability (emergency coordination) but unsustainable over longer periods without at least partial convergence emerging. This connects back to Tension 4.

**What this means for the thesis:** The boundary conditions section is potentially the most original contribution of the thesis. If the formal apparatus can characterise *where* structural coordination fails as precisely as where it succeeds, that tells us something deep about the nature of coordination itself: not just that it can proceed without convergence, but what it *actually requires* at the most fundamental level.

## Tension 6: Objective Structure vs. Subjective Experience of Structure

**Where it appears:** Surfaced during reading of Bradley (2022) "Social Ethics under Ambiguity," but applies across the entire formal programme. Bradley's framework collapses what is good for an individual and what the evaluator judges to be good into a single well-being measure (requiring "well-informed and of clear mind" epistemic conditions). The coordination space proposal separates these.

**The distinction:** Structural features of interdependence (shared risk, entanglement, constraints, procedural commitments) are objective properties of a configuration. They are facts about the world: how connected agents are, what risks they share, what constraints bind them. These do not change depending on who is looking.

What changes is how agents perceive and weight those features from their position. Australia and the UK face the same objective risk topology in AUKUS, but their proximity, history and strategic doctrine make the same objective risk feel different. The structure is the same. The experience of the structure is perspective-dependent.

**Why it matters:** This is a formal architecture question. The coordination space has two layers:

- **Layer 1 (objective):** The structural configuration. Measurable, perspective-independent. This is where stability is defined.
- **Layer 2 (subjective):** How each agent reads the configuration from their position. Perspective-dependent. This is where coordination behaviour (whether agents actually coordinate) comes from.

Most existing frameworks (Bradley's social evaluation, game theory's utility functions) collapse these layers into one. The thesis keeps them separate. The objective structure grounds the stability analysis. The subjective perception explains why agents do or don't coordinate in practice. They can diverge, and the divergence is itself informative.

**The strong claim:** Under what objective structural configurations is coordination stable regardless of how agents subjectively weight the features? If such configurations exist, the coordination space provides a stability result that doesn't require knowing what agents think, only what's true about their interdependence. This is a radically lower epistemic bar than any existing framework.

**The risk:** If subjective perception of structure always mediates whether coordination actually occurs, then the objective layer alone is insufficient for practical coordination (even if formally stable). The thesis would need to account for perception as well, re-introducing something like the agent capacities from Tension 1.

**Candidate resolution:** The two layers are complementary. Layer 1 tells you where coordination CAN be stable (structural possibility). Layer 2 tells you where it WILL be stable (behavioural realisation). The thesis contribution is Layer 1. The practice spine and operational insights contribute to Layer 2. Both are needed, but they're different research questions.

**Grounding references:**
- Nagel, T. *The View from Nowhere* (Oxford UP, 1986). The foundational critique of collapsing objective and subjective perspectives. The two-layer architecture is a formal implementation of Nagel's insight.
- Rowe and Voorhoeve, "Egalitarianism under Severe Uncertainty" *Philosophy and Public Affairs* 46 (2018). Engages with impartial vs situated evaluation under ambiguity. Voorhoeve is at LSE.
- Nowak and May (1992). Lattice topology determines outcomes regardless of agent beliefs. Layer 1 doing work without Layer 2.
- Schelling, *The Strategy of Conflict* (1960). Focal points as objective structural features that coordinate without explicit reasoning.
- Sen, *Development as Freedom* (Oxford UP, 1999). Objective capabilities replacing subjective utility. Structural parallel to structural position variables.
- Bergemann and Morris, "Robust Mechanism Design" *Econometrica* 73(6), 2005. Mechanisms that work regardless of agents' beliefs. The mechanism-design version of the Layer 1 question.

## Notes on Resolution Strategy

These tensions share a common structure: the Unity Compass repository makes broad philosophical claims; the V2 research proposal investigates a specific formal question. The tensions arise because the broad claims sometimes presuppose answers to the specific question.

The resolution strategy is consistent across all five:

1. Let the formal investigation lead. V2's hypothesis is testable. The repo's claims are not (yet). Prioritise the investigation over the commitments.
2. Reframe repo claims as phenomena to be explained rather than axioms to build from. The capacities, the scale-independence claim, the convergence-as-byproduct language: all of these become *questions the investigation addresses* rather than *premises the investigation assumes*.
3. Use the tensions as sources of insight. Each tension points toward a genuine philosophical question that the thesis could address. They are features of a developing research programme, not bugs in a finished framework.

The repo and the thesis are not the same project. The repo is exploratory, public-facing, built to evolve. The thesis is formal, bounded, built to survive examination. The tensions between them are productive precisely because they mark the distance between philosophical ambition and philosophical rigour. The thesis is where the ambition gets tested.
