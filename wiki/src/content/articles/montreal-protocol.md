The **Montreal Protocol** (1987) coordinated 197 nations to phase out ozone-depleting substances. The ozone layer is measurably recovering. No comparable climate agreement has achieved equivalent results. The investigation treats it as the strongest available evidence that coordination across genuine difference at planetary scale is possible.

## What happened

Nations with radically different economic systems, political ideologies and development priorities coordinated on a shared environmental threat without requiring ideological convergence. The Protocol used phased implementation with built-in review periods, and a Multilateral Fund to address the asymmetric costs between developed and developing nations.

## Why it matters for the investigation

The Montreal Protocol is analysed through the investigation's coordination taxonomy. The problem structure was an assurance game rather than a pure social dilemma. Nations were not primarily tempted to free-ride on others' reductions. They were uncertain whether others would follow through. The Protocol's phased implementation directly addressed this: each phase built confidence for the next.

The Multilateral Fund removed the chicken dynamic that could have emerged between developed and developing nations. And the science was clear enough to prevent the problem from being reframed as [[Value Incommensurability]]: nobody was arguing that ozone depletion was a valid trade-off against other values.

## Contrast with climate coordination

Climate coordination faces a fundamentally different strategic structure. The temptation to free-ride is stronger (energy is more economically central than refrigerants). The costs are more diffuse and asymmetric. The science, while clear on direction, involves longer timescales and more complex feedback loops. And the problem does involve genuine value incommensurability: nations hold different views on the relative weight of economic development, environmental preservation and intergenerational justice.

## Transferable insight

Successful planetary coordination requires correctly diagnosing the strategic structure and designing mechanisms that match it. The Montreal Protocol succeeded because the coordination mechanism was precisely calibrated to the assurance-game structure of the problem. Climate coordination has consistently applied mechanisms designed for social dilemmas to a problem that increasingly looks like value incommensurability.
