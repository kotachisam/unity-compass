# Research Proposal: Coordination Without Convergence — Navigating the Structural Middle of Inter-Agent Interdependence

**Applicant:** Samuel Norton
**Degree:** MPhil/PhD in Philosophy, London School of Economics

> **Central claim.** Coordination theory has been built on three assumptions, often conflated: that agents must share an ideological framework, that their specific values must converge enough for shared action, or that their different values must be commensurable enough for aggregation. Most real coordination operates in territory where none of these hold cleanly. This dissertation defends the model-adequacy claim that the structural middle between these limits is the substantive ground of coordination in practice, articulates the structural features of interdependence that operate there, and investigates whether those features admit formal characterisation.

---

## 1. The Problem

*Target: 250-300 words*

**Purpose.** Frame the question of inter-agent coordination as philosophical, not game-theoretic. Open with the three assumptions the literature has variously made about what coordination requires: shared ideological framework, value convergence, or commensurable values. These assumptions are typically run together but are philosophically distinct, operating at different levels of abstraction (political philosophy, social choice, decision theory respectively). Each has been independently contested. None has been jointly addressed as part of the same framing problem.

**Literature to cite.**

- Ideological homogeneity assumption: communitarian readings (MacIntyre *After Virtue*, Sandel), critics within liberalism (Rawls *Political Liberalism* on overlapping consensus, Gaus *The Open Society and Its Complexities*).
- Value convergence assumption: standard game theory's aligned-preferences models, Bratman on shared cooperative activity and shared intentions.
- Commensurability assumption: standard utilitarianism, Arrow's social welfare functions, Klocksiem "In Defense of the Trichotomy Thesis," Dorr/Nebel/Zuehl "The Case for Comparability."

---

## 2. The Central Claim

*Target: 200-250 words*

**Purpose.** State the model-adequacy thesis cleanly. The three assumptions form a useful theoretical scaffold but a vastly inaccurate model of coordination as it actually occurs. The substantive territory is the structural middle. Defending this claim and articulating that middle is the dissertation's primary work. Position the move as constructive (build a better model) not merely critical (the existing model is wrong). Position-tag the thesis as parallel to but distinct from the intra-agent trichotomy debate: just as the intra-agent better/worse/equal trichotomy has been contested as inadequate for individual rational choice under value plurality, the inter-agent assumptions of homogeneity/convergence/commensurability are inadequate for collective coordination.

**Literature to cite.**

- Model-adequacy framing: Box "All models are wrong" (Box & Draper 1987), Cartwright *How the Laws of Physics Lie*, Frigg & Hartmann SEP "Models in Science," Frigg & Nguyen 2020 *Models and Representation*.
- Parallel intra-agent debate: Chang *Comparativism: The Ground of Rational Choice*, Rabinowicz "Value Relations," Sen "Maximization and the Act of Choice."

## 3. What's in the Structural Middle

*Target: 300-350 words*

**Purpose.** Philosophical articulation of structural features that operate in the middle: shared risk topology, structural entanglement, procedural commitments, network position. These are not reducible to "shared ideology," "shared values," or "commensurable values." Use concrete cases to motivate each feature. This is descriptive philosophy, not formal modelling. The candidate features are working hypotheses, not a closed list, and identifying which features actually do load-bearing coordination work versus which are epiphenomenal is itself part of the research programme.

**Literature to cite.**

- Ostrom *Governing the Commons* (coordination through institutional design without value or ideological convergence).
- Schelling *The Strategy of Conflict* (focal points and structural salience).
- Olson *The Logic of Collective Action* (shared interests insufficient for coordination — the negative case that motivates structural features).
- Alexander *The Structural Evolution of Morality* (coordination norms emerging from structure rather than prior agreement).
- Network theory hooks if relevant: Granovetter on weak ties, Watts on networked coordination.
- Bratman on shared cooperative activity (cited as the position that grounds coordination in shared intentions — your move is that the structural co-occurrence with shared intentions does the work, not the intentions themselves).

## 4. The Stake

*Target: 200-250 words*

**Purpose.** Why this matters beyond academia. Keep abstract and implicit. Do not make civilisational claims. The substantive stake is challenging the philosophical foundations of how coordination has been theorised across three literatures (political philosophy, social choice, decision theory). The convergence-or-commensurability-or-homogeneity framing is what locks practical political philosophy into agreement-or-conflict patterns. A more adequate model of coordination opens space for approaches that respect ineliminable structural diversity in agents' evaluations. Note: the long-arc downstream implications (unity-respecting governance, multi-stakeholder coordination across genuine difference) are obvious enough that they don't need to be asserted; asserting them in a PhD proposal would be hubristic.

**Literature to cite.**

- Berlin "Two Concepts of Liberty" (pluralism without relativism).
- Anderson *Value in Ethics and Economics* (pragmatic incommensurability and what it costs to ignore).
- Hsieh & Andersson SEP "Incommensurable Values" (the unresolved status of the relevant philosophical debates).

## 5. The AUKUS Case

*Target: 250-300 words*

**Purpose.** Concrete instance of coordination in the structural middle. The UK, USA and Australia operate under structurally different strategic doctrines, democratic constraints, and definitions of regional security. They coordinate effectively in practice. Frame the case as evidence that the three assumptions all fail to fit. Ideological homogeneity fails (different democratic and security ideologies). Value convergence fails (different security values). Commensurability fails (no common payoff structure can be constructed for a common-or-garden game-theoretic model). The case is evidence the structural middle is real, not illustration of a hypothetical. Use operational experience to ground the case in detail rather than treating it as a stylised example. Acknowledge confirmation-bias risk by committing to test against one secondary domain without operational familiarity (likely climate governance or AI regulation, pick one).

**Literature to cite.**

- AUKUS-specific policy literature (this is your operational territory, not philosophical).
- Schelling on great-power coordination as a comparator case.
- Bradley & Spiekermann if their Open Society work on cohesion under severe uncertainty is relevant.

## 6. The Research Programme

*Target: 200-250 words*

**Purpose.** What the PhD actually does. Defends the philosophical claim that the three assumptions form a useful but inadequate model. Characterises the structural middle through philosophical articulation and case-driven probing. Identifies boundary conditions: the degree of structural entanglement below which interdependence cannot ground coordination, and the conditions under which divergence on all three axes is sufficiently total that no structural feature provides purchase. Whether the middle admits a formal apparatus with existence results comparable to Nash's is itself a substantive research question the thesis investigates, not a deliverable it promises. Honest scoping per Alexander's note that existence-of-equilibrium for any proposed concept could itself be the main result of the thesis: this is acknowledged, and the proposal is framed accordingly. The philosophical contribution is primary; the formal apparatus is one possible downstream output.

**Literature to cite.**

- Sen *Maximization and the Act of Choice* (formal alternative to optimisation under incomplete orderings, as exemplar that formal-but-incomplete apparatuses exist).
- Levi *Hard Choices* (V-admissibility as another exemplar).
- Dietrich and List 2017 "What Matters and How It Matters" (reason-based representation as formal apparatus for moral theories — LSE-relevant).
- Bradley 2022 "Impartial Evaluation under Ambiguity" (the "left for another day" hook on incomplete betterness relations).
- Alexander's email correspondence (personal communication 9 May 2026) on existence-of-equilibrium as viable thesis.

## Drafting guardrails

Things to keep out:

- Any "this thesis will deliver characterisation results" language. Existence and characterisation are open research questions.
- Generalising to climate + AI + financial coordination in one breath. Pick one secondary case to triangulate against AUKUS.
- Heavy Bradley, Gaus, Bratman engagement in the proposal body. These belong in footnotes or in a brief supervisor-engagement note.
- Bracketing Alexander's corpus as the methodology frame. The framing is mine; his work is referenced not central.
- Anything that makes this sound like a decision-theory paper rather than a philosophy paper.
- "Is false" claims about the assumptions. The move is model-adequacy, not logical falsification.
- Civilisational stakes asserted explicitly. The implications are obvious; asserting them is hubristic in a PhD proposal.
