**Evolutionary game theory** (EGT) extends classical game theory by modelling strategic interaction as a dynamic, adaptive process rather than a one-shot rational calculation. Where classical game theory asks what rational agents should do, EGT asks what strategies survive and spread in populations of agents who interact repeatedly and adapt over time.

## Key concepts

**Evolutionarily stable strategy (ESS)**: a strategy that, once adopted by a population, cannot be invaded by any mutant strategy. Introduced by John Maynard Smith and George Price (1973).

**Replicator dynamics**: the mathematical framework describing how the frequency of strategies in a population changes over time based on their relative fitness.

**Structured populations**: agents interact with neighbours on a network rather than with the entire population. The topology of the network affects which strategies spread. [[J. McKenzie Alexander]]'s work focuses extensively on how network structure shapes norm evolution.

## Relevance to the investigation

EGT is central to sub-question 2 of the research proposal: under what conditions do coordination equilibria grounded in [[Structural Interdependence]] resist defection, capture and decay?

The investigation's key question for EGT: Alexander's models require a constructible payoff structure. What happens when you relax that assumption? Can evolutionary stability analysis be extended to agents whose payoffs are genuinely incomparable? If structural features of interdependence can substitute for a common payoff matrix, what does evolutionary stability look like in that regime?

## Limitations for the investigation

EGT inherits classical game theory's foundational requirement: a constructible payoff structure. The agents in EGT models are playing a defined game with numerical payoffs. The Hawk-Dove game with its fixed value V, for instance, appears to depend on V being a number that both players agree represents the value of the resource. When they don't agree, the model has no starting point. This is the gap the investigation operates in.
